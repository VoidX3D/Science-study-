Unit 1: Scientific Study

Scientific study is a systematic way of understanding the natural world using observation, experimentation, and logical reasoning. This unit forms the foundation of all science.

1. Core Concept: Scientific Method

Observation

Scientific study begins with careful observation of natural phenomena. An observation may raise curiosity or a question that requires explanation.

Hypothesis

A hypothesis is a testable assumption based on prior knowledge. It predicts a possible outcome.

Experimentation

Experiments are controlled tests designed to verify or reject the hypothesis. Accurate instruments and repeated trials are essential.

Analysis and Conclusion

Collected data are analyzed using tables, graphs, and calculations. Conclusions are drawn based on evidence.

Reporting

Results are shared through reports, journals, or presentations so others can verify or build upon the findings.

2. Branches of Science

  • Physics: Study of matter, energy, force, motion, and the universe.
  • Chemistry: Study of substances, their properties, and chemical reactions.
  • Biology: Study of living organisms and life processes.

3. Contributions of Science

  • Development of medicine and vaccines
  • Communication technologies
  • Transportation and space exploration
  • Agricultural improvements

4. Laboratory Safety Rules

  • Always wear protective equipment like lab coats and goggles.
  • Never taste or directly smell chemicals.
  • Heat flammable liquids using a water bath, not direct flame.
  • Add acid to water slowly, never water to acid.
  • Handle electrical equipment with dry hands.
